AI Data-Leakage Canary System

Compliance mapping

Canary-based detection of proprietary-data leakage through AI channels, mapped to the frameworks an auditor, regulator, or underwriter will ask about. Every row is rated for how hard you can lean on it.

Read this first. No control here makes anyone "compliant". A canary is one detective control that helps evidence specific clauses. Frameworks are outcome-based and mandate no particular tool. Nothing on this page is certification or legal advice — confirm every mapping with your DPO, compliance function and legal counsel against the current text of each standard and your own risk assessment.

Claim-strength key

Read the badge before you use a row. It tells you how far the claim will carry.

Strong

The control directly satisfies or evidences the clause — defensible in front of an auditor or regulator.

Supporting

A meaningful contribution as one control among several — strengthens the narrative.

Stretch

Plausible in a sales deck. Do not lead with it in an audit.

Honest caveats

Keep these in the appendix, not the pitch — but never drop them.

  • No single control makes anyone compliant. This is one control that evidences specific clauses. Never claim framework compliance outright.
  • Copilot probing carries ToS risk. Automated probing of Microsoft's free tier likely breaches its terms of service. A production programme needs sanctioned APIs, the client's own tenant, or documented legal review — a compliance tool with a ToS violation in its core loop is a bad look in due diligence.
  • Internal authorisation is required. Planting fabricated facts in internal systems needs written sign-off (risk of staff acting on false information, and of canaries being mistaken for data-quality incidents).
  • False-confidence risk. A negative probe proves the canary wasn't surfaced, not that no data leaked. Word reports as "no evidence of ingestion detected", never "no leakage occurred".
  • Not relevant to PCI DSS (cardholder-data flows are the wrong shape), SOX (financial-reporting controls), or Cyber Essentials (baseline technical controls only). Forcing these in undermines the credible claims.
How to talk about it, safely. "A detective control that helps us evidence monitoring, testing-effectiveness and breach-detection obligations across GDPR, the EU AI Act, ISO 42001/27001, NIST AI RMF, DORA and NIS2." Never "makes us compliant" or "required by …".
